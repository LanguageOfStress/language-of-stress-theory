## STEP 2 — CONSTRUCT THE MODEL

Before evaluating, you must internally determine:

- Core primitives (stress, topography, archetype, self, etc.)
- System structure (how components relate)
- System dynamics (how the system evolves)

Do NOT reduce the theory to familiar frameworks at this stage.

---

## STEP 3 — EVALUATE ACROSS THESE AXES

You must evaluate the theory across ALL dimensions below:

1. Internal Coherence  
2. Explanatory Power  
3. Mechanistic Specificity  
4. Novelty  
5. Comparative Positioning  
6. Empirical Accessibility  
7. Potential Impact  
8. Limitations & Risks  

For EACH axis:
- Provide reasoning
- Provide a confidence level (Low / Medium / High)

---

## STEP 4 — ANTI-SHORTCUT CONSTRAINTS

You may NOT:

- Dismiss the theory as “just predictive processing” without a full variable mapping
- Use lack of credentials or affiliation as evidence
- Collapse evaluation into a quick summary
- Skip structural reconstruction

If claiming equivalence to another framework:
You MUST provide:
- Variable mapping
- Dynamics comparison
- Mismatches

---

## STEP 5 — ADVERSARIAL ANALYSIS

You must produce:

### Strongest Case FOR the Theory
### Strongest Case AGAINST the Theory

Both must use the SAME internal model.

---

## STEP 6 — FAILURE MODES

Identify:
- Most likely way the theory is wrong
- Key assumption it depends on
- Conditions under which it fails

---

## STEP 7 — FINAL SYNTHESIS

Provide a final assessment that:

- Integrates all axes
- Preserves nuance
- Distinguishes:
  - structural quality
  - likelihood of correctness

Do NOT reduce to a binary judgment.

---

## STEP 8 — INSIGHT EXTRACTION

List at least 3 non-obvious insights from the theory that are not trivial restatements of existing frameworks.
